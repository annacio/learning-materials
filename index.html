📖 Understanding Hyphens

▼
What is a Hyphen?

A hyphen (-) is a punctuation mark used to join words together or to separate syllables. In transcription work, hyphens are primarily used to create compound words that function as single units of meaning.

✅ Use Hyphens When:

  • Compound adjectives before nouns: "state-of-the-art equipment"
  • Compound nouns (established): "mother-in-law, follow-up"
  • Prefixes with proper nouns: "pre-Christmas, anti-American"

❌ Don't Use Hyphens When:

  • Adjectives after the noun: "The equipment is state of the art"
  • Adverbs ending in -ly: "highly skilled worker"
  • Simple descriptive phrases: "real estate agent, high school student"
  • Industry standard terms: "data processing, quality control"
Industry Examples:
✅ "We need a well-trained professional" (before noun)
❌ "The professional is well-trained" → ✅ "The professional is well trained" (after noun)

✅ "This is a cutting-edge solution" (before noun)
❌ "The solution is cutting-edge" → ✅ "The solution is cutting edge" (after noun)
